Mararet Hamilton

Margaret Hamilton was a woman. Born in 1936, she lived through World War II and the Cold War, up until the present. She has seen the floppy disk come and go along with many other things. She could tell me so many interesting stories, which is what made me interested in writing about her. While looking her up, I found out that she actually studied math, which I rarely find amusing or interesting. So my faith in becoming like her plummeted. But I continued reading.

Before

So, she is an American. She was a great IT person and worked on getting people to the moon. But what did she do before that?
Although we call her a computer scientist, she actually studied math. She liked abstract math. Which I don't EVEN understand WHAT it is.
She first studied mathematics at the University of Michigan in 1955 before transferring to Earlham College, where her mother had been a student. She earned her BA there and then went on to study abstract mathematics at Brandeis University. You might think she would stay there and get a degree in abstract math. But no—she went to MIT to work for a meteorology professor.So that's how she ended up working on sending people to the moon. By now, you're probably wondering where the computer science part of all this is. Well, I was too. So hier is It. The computer science of it. while working for the meteorology professor, she developed a Soft-Ware that predicts weather. After that, but still at MIT Magaret Hamelton programmed some software for the US air force.

While

What did she do in the Apollo project? Was she that important?
Well, Yes. She was that important. She was the first programmer on the project. She made the software prioritize things, wich directly made it posible to continue the landing. She led the team that wrote and tested all the flight software for both the Command Module and the Lunar Module, making sure everything worked perfectly during the missions. She also created systems that could detect and fix errors automatically, which meant the astronauts could stay safe even when something went wrong. During the Apollo 11 mission, when the computer was overloaded with alarms, her software's smart recovery system kept it running instead of crashing. Because of that, the landing could continue. She also came up with ways to restart programs safely and make the system more reliable. Her “priority display” showed the astronauts the most important warnings first, helping them make the right decisions in critical moments. Thanks to her work, the Apollo software was extremely reliable and helped ensure the success of the Moon landing.

After

After the Apollo project she still did a lot of things. Some of things that I find interesting are the number of papers that she published. Also she Co-founded Higher Order Software (HOS), with the intention of developing new methods for error prevention and fault tolerance. Using the knowinge from the apollo project.
