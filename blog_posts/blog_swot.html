S.W.O.T.

strengths

One of my biggest strengths is curiosity. Whether it's programming an Arduino or diving into history, I like to know how things work. I also tend to be persistent — if I don't understand something right away, I'll keep digging until it makes sense. Another strength is honesty in communication. I prefer being clear and direct, even if it feels blunt, because it helps avoid misunderstandings.

weaknesses

At the same time, I know I struggle with collaboration. Group work doesn't always come naturally to me, and I sometimes overthink what others might mean in conversations. Picking up on social cues can be difficult, which makes me second-guess myself and sometimes stay quiet instead of speaking up. I can also be overly self-critical, replaying moments in my head instead of letting them go.

opportunities

Despite these challenges, I see many opportunities. I'm interested in software engineering, which gives me room to apply my problem-solving skills and creativity. I also have opportunities to grow in my social life — for example, I've been building closer connections with friends and I've been learning how to express what kind of support works for me. Being half-Korean also gives me a unique cultural perspective that I can use in both school and personal projects.

threats

The biggest threats I face are internal: anxiety, self-doubt, and perfectionism. These can hold me back from taking risks or fully enjoying the progress I make. There's also the risk of miscommunication in friendships and relationships, since I don't always interpret signals the same way others do. On top of that, external pressures like exams and school deadlines can add extra stress.
